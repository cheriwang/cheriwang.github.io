NCXT


User experience research & design internship with NCXT, a digital strategy and transformations company, for a client.

Role: User Experience Design & Research Intern

12 weeks

overview


This past summer, I had the opportunity to intern with NCXT, a consulting group in Minneapolis that manages digital transformations and products through a human centered and data driven approach. Over the course of my 12-week internship I primarily worked with one of our clients, MoneyVerbs.

MoneyVerbs is a financial learning startup created to help users grow their financial knowledge through community based discussions, challenges and accomplishments, and personal goal setting.

MY ROLE


I held the position of User Experience Design & Research Intern. I worked closely with the MoneyVerbs software development team in a two-week, sprint-based, agile environment, as well as with the lo-fi and hi-fi designers to implement design changes in Figma. Additionally, through research, I helped brainstorm, reimagine, and gain insight on new features on the app to increase user engagement.

structure


Every two weeks, using our development roadmap, I helped define the sprint research goals and design prototypes, planned and conducted usability tests, synthesized and delivered insights and recommendations for future design decisions.

Test & Synthesis


After creating the wireframes, I wrote a test plan defining the tasks and questions in preparation for user testing. During each session, I instructed the participants to click through the tasks outlined in the test plan, and took note on pathways the participants took, problems experienced, comments and recommendations, and answers to open-ended questions for current and future features.

After each session, I synthesized the insights and observations, and presented a slide deck with an overview of the sprint and recommendations to the marketing, design, and developer team to implement in the current and future wireframes. Below are the some of the deliverables I would present.
